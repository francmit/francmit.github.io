Our Story

First Met

Fall 2014

Janine and Mitchell met during the first quarter of their freshman year at the University of Washington in an introductory astronomy class. They began getting together for study sessions, which quickly turned into weekend hang outs and even a first date to Tofu House – which, to this day, is still their favorite Korean restaurant.

Started Dating

April 18, 2015

After a few months of weekend hang outs, Janine and Mitchell started dating in spring quarter of their freshman year. Before going on their separate educational paths, the two tried to take some classes together and finally managed to get into the same film class. This meant they got to spend some time together during the weekdays as well, which was beneficial for both of them.

Japan Long Distance Relationship

July 2018 – July 2019

With Mitchell’s support, four years into the relationship, Janine left to live in Japan and fulfill her lifelong ambition of teaching English. The two went into a long-distance relationship for a year and managed to keep their relationship strong through thousands of Discord messages, numerous weekend phone calls despite the 16-hour time difference, and one week-long vacation for Mitchell to visit. The time apart was not easy, but it allowed them to grow, as individuals and as a couple, even more.

Engaged

February 12, 2022

On a gorgeous and sunny February afternoon in Magnuson Park, Mitchell found a quiet place on the water to pop the question, and of course, Janine said yes. The date ended with a classic: a trip to Tofu House. After almost seven years of dating and over a year of engagement, the two will spend their lifetime together on July 8th, 2023.

Questions

When should I RSVP by?
Please RSVP no later than 5/31/2023. If you are unable to respond by then we will assume you can't make it.
Are you registered? Where?
Check out the registry tab on this wedding website! Otherwise, feel free to look up our registries on Amazon under either of our names.
Is there a dress code?
We'd love our guests to sport comfortable semi-formal attire. Ladies can wear a nice dress, and the men can wear dress shirt/pants (tie optional). Ladies, the venue does have grass and other unstable terrain, so stiletto heels may not be the best choice of footwear.
Do you have wedding colors?
Our main color is dusty blue. Other colors we've chosen are navy blue, ivory/off white, and sage green. Please note however that there is no color dress code, so you do not need to wear something that matches these shades.
Are kids welcome?
If they are included on the invitation, then of course! Only kids from our families can be accommodated, so thank you for understanding. Also, there is a considerable amount of space to play on grass and wood chips, so bringing a change of clothes for them might be advisable.
Do I need to wear a mask?
You do not need to wear one, but you may if you wish.
Is there parking for the ceremony and reception?
Yes, there is plenty of parking on site at Craven Farm.
What time should I arrive at the ceremony?
The ceremony begins at 4:00 p.m. Please arrive by 3:45 p.m. to ensure you get a good seat!
I have a dietary restriction/food preference. Can you accommodate me?
Please make sure to indicate your dietary needs when you RSVP and we will do our best to work with our caterer to make sure there is something available for you at the reception.
